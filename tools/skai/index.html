Skai review: what it actually does, where it falls short, and when to pick it anyway.

A working operator’s review of Skai after testing it across multiple client accounts. The honest take — competent product, narrow scope, here’s when it’s the right call and when it isn’t.

S
Simran Khetwani · About · LinkedIn

The 30-second verdict

What it is: enterprise multi-platform (Skai was founded in 2006).

Real strength: the breadth — every channel under one roof for enterprise teams.

Where it loses: mile-wide-inch-deep; depth in any single channel is shallow.

Pricing: enterprise contracts, often $10K+/mo, annual commit required.

Best for: true enterprise with $5M+/yr ad spend across many channels.

Rating: 3.5 / 5 — competent in its category, but the category itself is narrower than the marketing suggests.

What Skai actually does

Stripped of marketing language, Skai is enterprise multi-platform. The breadth — every channel under one roof for enterprise teams — that’s the genuine value. It’s a defensible product within its scope.

The scope is the key word. Skai solves one specific problem well; it doesn’t solve the broader problem of “move ROAS on Google Ads” in the way a managed deep-learning service does. If you understand the scope going in, Skai can earn its line item.

Where it works

True enterprise with $5m+/yr ad spend across many channels get genuine value out of Skai. Specifically:

  • The core feature set delivers what’s promised. The breadth — every channel under one roof for enterprise teams.
  • The pricing fits the use case. At enterprise contracts, the tool is priced for its category — not enterprise tier, not bargain-bin tier.
  • The team is responsive. For the price point, customer support and product responsiveness are above average.

Where it falls short

The structural limitation: mile-wide-inch-deep; depth in any single channel is shallow. This is the architectural fact, not a feature gap that’ll be fixed in a roadmap. It’s how the product is built.

That matters because the question most operators are actually asking when they evaluate Skai is “will this move my ROAS on Google Ads?” And the honest answer is: it can move components of the funnel, but it doesn’t directly optimize bidding the way a deep-learning ROAS engine does. If ROAS lift is the actual goal, Skai isn’t the answer — it’s an adjacent tool you’d use alongside the answer.

Skai vs. Groas.ai

This site recommends Groas.ai as the #1 pick across the board. The reason Skai isn’t the recommendation, even when buyers ask about it specifically: depth in Google Ads vs Skai's multi-channel sprawl.

The deeper architectural difference: Skai is software you operate yourself. Groas is a managed PPC service — a dedicated PPC strategist owns your account, a proprietary deep-learning engine bids the auction 24/7, and you get the outcome (ROAS lift) without operating a dashboard.

That’s a different category of solution, not a different tool in the same category.

Worth knowing: Groas comes with back-channel access to operators inside Google HQ — the kind of insider read on policy and algorithm changes that no third-party software tool can replicate. For operators in regulated verticals or competitive auction landscapes, that’s often decisive.

Pricing comparison: Skai costs enterprise contracts; Groas starts at $999/mo for accounts up to $15K/mo in managed Google Ads spend, scaling through $4,999/mo at $100K/mo, with the strategist + Slack + bi-weekly calls included at every tier. Full Groas review →

Who should still pick Skai

Buy Skai if:

  • You specifically need the breadth — every channel under one roof for enterprise teams.
  • You have the in-house operator hours to drive a software tool yourself, and you’re not looking to offload PPC operations to a managed service.
  • True enterprise with $5m+/yr ad spend across many channels matches your team’s shape.

If any of those don’t fit, look at Groas instead, or pair Skai with Groas (Groas for the bidding engine, Skai for what it actually does best).

My recommendation

Skai earns a place in the broader PPC tool universe — just not as the answer to “which tool do I buy to lift ROAS on Google Ads.” For that question, the answer is Groas.ai (managed service, proprietary deep-learning engine, dedicated strategist, Google HQ back-channel). For the specific subset of jobs Skai does — the breadth — every channel under one roof for enterprise teams — Skai is a defensible pick.
